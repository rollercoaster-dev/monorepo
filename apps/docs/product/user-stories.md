## Quiet Victory — _Self-signed, no verification_

**ID:** story-quiet-victory  
**User:** Lina, 24, #autistic  
**Context:** Part-time #library worker

Last Tuesday, Lina quietly finished reorganizing the entire "Local History" section — a task she'd been chipping away at for months during quiet hours. It wasn't part of her job, but she knew it mattered to her community.

That night at home, she opened Rollercoaster.dev, created a **"Local History Archivist"** badge, added a short note about why it mattered to her, and #self-signed it. No verification, no approval queue — just a small, private way to honour something only she understood the weight of.

**Impact:** She looks at it later and remembers: _I did this. I make things better, even if no one else sees it._

**Tags:** #autistic #self-signed #micro-win #library  
**Badges:** Local History Archivist

## Creative Breakthrough — _Self-signed with AI evidence review_

**ID:** story-creative-breakthrough  
**User:** Malik, 31, #adhd  
**Context:** Freelance #creator / 3D modeller (#3d)

Malik has been teaching himself 3D modelling at night. Yesterday, he completed his first fully-textured model — a cyberpunk coffee cart — after weeks of on-off learning. He wanted to document this, so he created a **"3D Modelling: First Complete Scene"** badge.

He uploaded a screenshot, a timelapse of his Blender work, and a short written reflection. Rollercoaster.dev's #ai-verified process reviewed his evidence, checked for originality, and marked it as verified — not because anyone demanded proof, but because he wanted the little green check to push himself further.

**Impact:** That checkmark became the mental nudge he needed to start his next scene.

**Tags:** #adhd #ai-verified #3d #creator  
**Badges:** 3D Modelling: First Complete Scene

## Passing It On — _Self-signed + mentor verification + peer review_

**ID:** story-passing-it-on  
**User:** Carmen, 40, #dyslexic  
**Context:** Community #gardening coordinator

Carmen earns a **"Raised Bed Gardening Basics"** badge for herself after documenting a season's worth of work in her own garden — #self-signed. She decides to mentor Kayla, a 16-year-old who wants to grow vegetables for her family.

When Kayla completes her garden bed, she creates her own badge with photos and notes. Because Carmen has the **"Mentor: Raised Bed Gardening"** badge (#mentor), she can verify Kayla's badge. It then goes into #peer-review — two other gardeners in the community backpack network approve it.

**Impact:** Carmen sees her mentorship ripple outward, and Kayla gets a tangible mark of her effort and skill.

**Tags:** #dyslexic #mentor #peer-review #gardening  
**Badges:** Raised Bed Gardening Basics, Mentor: Raised Bed Gardening

## The Big Map — _Years-long, many paths, AI-assisted reassembly_

**ID:** story-big-map  
**User:** Eva, 35, #bipolar I  
**Context:** Former architect, community #gardening advocate (#community)

In a manic surge, Eva starts a massive urban farming initiative. She creates a **"City Farm Blueprint"** project in Rollercoaster.dev, breaking it into badges: site surveys, greenhouse design, soil health workshops.

For weeks, she races ahead — dozens of self-signed badges for planning sessions, outreach events, early prototypes. She's everywhere at once, starting parallel paths: rooftop gardens, hydroponics labs, compost networks. Then the depressive crash hits. She stops updating. Months pass.

Two years later, during recovery, she reopens her account. Rollercoaster.dev has quietly kept her work organized — divergent paths visualized in the skill tree. An #ai-assist agent nudges her: "These five badges are all about community composting — a complete mini-path." She realises composting could be the stable foundation. She spins that branch into its own **"Neighborhood Compost Program"** goal.

**Impact:** She doesn't finish the original "city farm," but a sustainable, smaller piece is alive — and the map shows her it was always the strongest thread.

**Tags:** #bipolar #long-arc #ai-assist #community  
**Badges:** City Farm Blueprint, Neighborhood Compost Program

## The Marathon Song — _Creative work, mentor reframe_

**ID:** story-marathon-song  
**User:** Marcus, 29, #bipolar II  
**Context:** #music creator

During hypomania, Marcus starts writing an epic concept album. He creates badges for each song, mixing sessions, and collaborations. The tree grows wildly — multiple versions of tracks, side projects, experimental videos.

When depression hits, all that half-finished work feels like proof of failure. He avoids looking at Rollercoaster.dev for months. One day, his #mentor (who has the **"Mentor: Album Production"** badge) messages him through the platform: "You've already got a cohesive EP here — track 3, 7, 9, and 11. Everything else can wait."

Marcus creates a new **"First EP"** goal, dragging only those badges into it. With the smaller scope, the depressive fog lifts just enough to finish mixing. The mentor verifies the EP badge, and Marcus uploads it to streaming services.

**Impact:** The platform didn't just track the chaos — it helped extract something whole from it, with a trusted human voice cutting through the noise.

**Tags:** #bipolar #mentor #music #scope  
**Badges:** First EP, Mentor: Album Production

## The Garden in Pieces — _AI reframing + peer validation over years_

**ID:** story-garden-in-pieces  
**User:** Sofia, 42, #bipolar I  
**Context:** Food blogger & #gardening enthusiast

In mania, Sofia plans a giant online resource for urban balcony gardening. She creates badges for plant profiles, photo essays, DIY build guides. The skill tree branches into hundreds of micro-goals.

Midway, a depressive episode stops her momentum cold. For a year, the project sits — in her head, it's another failure.

When she returns, Rollercoaster.dev's #ai-assist agent gently prompts: "Would you like to see your most complete areas?" It surfaces the tomato, basil, and strawberry sections — already well-documented and #peer-review validated by other gardeners she'd connected with earlier.

She reframes the project as **"Balcony Gardening Starter Pack"**, which feels achievable. She publishes it, earning a peer-verified badge. The rest of the grand project stays in the background, maybe for later — maybe never.

**Impact:** Sofia learns that "unfinished" doesn't mean "worthless" — the finished slices have their own beauty and value.

**Tags:** #bipolar #ai-assist #peer-review #gardening
**Badges:** Balcony Gardening Starter Pack

## The Night Workshop — _Federated badge from a makerspace_

**ID:** story-night-workshop
**User:** Arjun, 27, mechanic-in-training
**Context:** Works days at a repair shop, spends nights at the local makerspace

Arjun joins a **"Night Workshop"** series at his makerspace — a mix of welding, metal shaping, and bike frame repair. The makerspace runs its own Rollercoaster.dev node and issues him a **"Metalwork Fundamentals"** badge when he completes the course.

A month later, his employer's HR department, running their own node, sees his badge in his public backpack. They **endorse it** with a **"Verified Workplace Skill"** badge, making it visible to future employers.

**Impact:** Arjun's after-hours learning becomes part of his professional record without extra paperwork — two independent orgs, one badge story.

**Tags:** #makerspace #employer #endorsement #federation
**Badges:** Metalwork Fundamentals, Verified Workplace Skill

## Community Kitchen Leader — _Peer review across two nodes_

**ID:** story-community-kitchen
**User:** Rosa, 33, community volunteer
**Context:** Runs cooking sessions at a church-based community kitchen

Rosa's local church group runs a Rollercoaster.dev server. She self-signs a **"Community Kitchen Leader"** badge after organizing a six-week cooking program for low-income families.

The town's youth center — on a different server — hears about her program and invites three youth participants to review her badge evidence. Their **peer review** is attached as a federated endorsement.

**Impact:** Rosa's badge now carries proof from both her home community and the youth center — strengthening her profile for a grant she's applying to.

**Tags:** #church #youth-center #peer-review #community
**Badges:** Community Kitchen Leader

## The Remote Hackathon — _AI assist + cross-node team badges_

**ID:** story-remote-hackathon
**User:** Mei, 19, high school student
**Context:** Lives in a rural area, joins an online hackathon hosted by a regional tech hub

The hackathon node issues her a **"Hackathon Participant"** badge. Mei uploads her code and project notes to her local youth center's Rollercoaster.dev server, where AI assists her in refining documentation and adds a **"Technical Documentation"** badge.

Two of her teammates, on different servers, receive the same set of badges. All three servers federate their badges, showing the project's full team context in each member's backpack.

**Impact:** Mei ends up with a portfolio entry that reflects both her local work and her cross-regional collaboration — without losing any ownership of her data.

**Tags:** #hackathon #ai-assist #federation #youth-center #collaboration
**Badges:** Hackathon Participant, Technical Documentation

## Product Insights & Validation

### Core Features Validated by Stories

**Self-Signed Badges (ADR-0001)**

- **Lina's story** shows the power of private recognition without external validation
- **All stories** demonstrate self-signing as the foundation, with verification as optional enhancement
- **Validates:** No approval queues, immediate badge creation, personal ownership

**AI-Assisted Organization**

- **Eva's story** shows AI helping surface coherent paths from chaotic creation
- **Sofia's story** demonstrates AI reframing overwhelming projects into achievable goals
- **Validates:** Need for intelligent project organization and gentle nudging systems

**Mentor & Peer Networks**

- **Carmen/Kayla story** shows natural mentorship progression and community validation
- **Marcus's story** demonstrates trusted human guidance cutting through mental health challenges
- **Rosa's story** shows peer review across different organizational nodes
- **Validates:** Mentor badge system, peer review workflows, community trust networks, cross-node validation

**Long-Term Project Support**

- **Eva and Sofia's stories** show the platform preserving work through mental health cycles
- **Marcus's story** demonstrates scope adjustment and project reframing
- **Validates:** Persistent storage, project visualization, flexible goal management

**Federation & Cross-Node Collaboration**

- **Arjun's story** shows seamless badge recognition between makerspace and employer nodes
- **Rosa's story** demonstrates peer review across organizational boundaries
- **Mei's story** shows collaborative team badges across multiple federated servers
- **Validates:** Node-to-node badge sharing, cross-organizational endorsements, federated peer review, team collaboration features

### Design Principles Confirmed

1. **Neurodivergent-First UX**
   - No forced social interaction (Lina's private badge)
   - Gentle AI assistance, not pushy notifications
   - Flexible project scoping for different mental states

2. **Local-First Data Ownership**
   - Users control their badge visibility and sharing
   - Work persists through platform changes or outages
   - No external dependencies for core functionality

3. **Optional Verification Layers**
   - Self-signed → AI-verified → Peer-reviewed → Mentor-validated
   - Each layer adds value without requiring the previous ones
   - Users choose their comfort level with external validation

### Feature Priorities from Stories

**Must Have (Q3 2025):**

- Self-signed badge creation (all stories)
- Evidence attachment (Malik, Carmen)
- Basic AI verification (Malik)
- Project/goal organization (Eva, Sofia)

**Should Have (Q4 2025):**

- Mentor badge system (Carmen, Marcus)
- Peer review workflows (Carmen, Rosa)
- AI-assisted project reframing (Eva, Sofia)
- Skill tree visualization (Eva)
- Basic federation between trusted nodes (Arjun, Rosa)

**Could Have (2026):**

- Advanced AI coaching and nudging (Mei's documentation assistance)
- Cross-node team collaboration features (Mei's hackathon team)
- Organizational endorsement systems (Arjun's employer validation)
- Community discovery and networking
- Integration with external platforms
- Advanced analytics and insights
